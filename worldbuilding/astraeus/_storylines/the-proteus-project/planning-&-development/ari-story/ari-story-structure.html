Ari Story Structure

Act 1

Hook

Introduction to Ari’s inner conflict

- What does Ari think will bring him true happiness or contentment? And how is his fear stopping him from going after it?

Ari want's to leave the scrapper guild. He wants to be a pilot and see the galaxy. He believes freedom will bring him true happiness. He fears his uncle Brax.

Also his friends are very important to him.

He also wants to find out about his healing powers and why Brax is so insistent he keeps them a secret.

Ari believes true happiness = freedom.

  • Leaving the scrapper guild

  • Becoming a pilot

  • Seeing the galaxy beyond Brax’s control

  • Living a life where he chooses his own path

    The fear stopping him:

  • Brax’s abuse and control

  • The belief that disobeying gets people killed (the misbelief)

  • Fear of failing his friends

  • Fear that leaving will harm the people he cares about or lead to tragedy like his parents’ deaths

This makes Ari emotionally stuck:
He wants the sky, but he's terrified he’ll destroy the people around him if he reaches for it.

- What is Ari’s misbelief and what happened in his past to create this misbelief?

His parents were killed by infected droids when he was young. Ari feels guilty for his parents' death. He believes that if he doesn't follow orders he can get people hurt.

Misbelief:

“If I disobey or act on my own, people die because of me.”

Origin:

When he was a child, he:

  • acted impulsively,
  • or broke formation,
  • or ran toward something he shouldn’t have,
  • or dragged his parents into danger
    → and infected droids killed them.

Even if it wasn’t actually his fault, he believes it was.

Brax weaponises this by constantly saying:

  • “Your parents died because you didn’t listen.”
  • “Look what happens when you don’t follow orders.”
  • “You’re going to get your friends killed if you keep acting stupid.”

Ari internalises:

  • Obey = safe
  • Disobey = death

Which directly conflicts with:

  • His desire for freedom
  • His dreams
  • His nature as a good person

He also has memories of his time in the lab, being experimented on.

- How can I show my audience in the FIRST FIVE MINUTES why “what’s happening” MATTERS to Ari, given his desire, fear, and misbelief?

Ari's impulsiveness alerts a rouge infected droid while he and his friends are scavenging. Reminds him of how it got his parents killed.

  • Action sequence.
  • Shows Ari's relationship with the other characters, how they are important to him.

1. Show Ari’s dream in action or conversation (1–2 paragraphs)

  • He stares up at a flight path and watches ships cross the sky

  • Or he fiddles with a broken nav-console like he knows exactly how to fix it

  • Or he tells Dom/Kai, jokingly but not really:
    “One day I’m getting off this rock.”

    2. Show the misbelief in real time (scavenging begins)

  • Ari gives strict warnings:
    “Stick close. Keep your comms open. Don’t wander.”

  • He’s bossy because he’s anxious

  • He’s anxious because he believes disobedience = death

    3. Show the moment where his desire and misbelief clash

Ari sees something cool—old ship parts, tech, something he always dreamed about.

He impulsively steps toward it.

Ari’s impulsiveness alerts a rogue infected droid.

Instant cause → effect.

4. Show the trauma reaction

When the droid wakes:

  • He freezes

  • He hears echoes of his parents screaming

  • He flashes back to the night they died

  • He panics and apologises out loud (“I’m sorry, I’m sorry—”) even though it’s irrational

  • He pulls Dom/Kai back, putting them behind him

  • His first instinct is to protect them, not himself

    5. Show why this moment matters

This proves to the audience:

  • He wants freedom
  • but thinks he doesn’t deserve it
  • because he believes he’ll get people killed
  • and this fear runs his entire life

And now the droid he accidentally alerted threatens the people he loves most.

This is emotionally and narratively perfect.

INCITING INCIDENT

PROTAGONIST IS PUSHED OUTSIDE COMFORT ZONE

- Why does this inciting incident matter to my protagonist? How does it push him outside his comfort zone? 

Ari finds out he's being sold to an underground science agency and might never see his friends again.

- What internal conflict is going to arise for my protagonist in wake of the inciting incident?

  • He wants to stay with his friends
  • He wants to get away from his scrapper life
  • He doesn't want to go with the agency

BUILD-UP

THE IMPOSSIBLE QUESTION

- What is the “impossible choice”? Your protagonist must be faced with options: stay inside their comfort zone and risk NEVER getting What They Desire, or venture into the unknown and accomplish their goal while STILL avoiding their fear.

The choice to escape with his friends and leave his life behind.

FIRST PINCH POINT

OPPOSITION/ANTAGONISTIC FORCE LOOMS IN THE DISTANCE

   - What is the opposition/antagonistic force my protagonist is going to have to face head-on later? How can I show the reader that it’s already looming in the distance?

He will need to face the agency, the agency show up and he escapes with Lila.

ACT 2

PRE-MIDPOINT REACTIONARY HERO

PROTAGONIST PURSUES THEIR FEAR-BASED GOAL

PROMPT:

Ask yourself:

- How is my protagonist going to execute their plan (achieve goal, avoid fear)?

He wants to figure out why the agency is after him and Lila

- What is their step-by-step plan for making this happen? (Even if they don’t know every step themselves).

They travel back to Sovari where they both were born, for answers.

- How can I show my reader the REASONING behind my protagonist’s actions (aka: how their every decision is fundamentally based on their misbelief)?

IDK BRUH

GAME-CHANGING MIDPOINT

PLOT TWIST!

- What unexpected thing is going to upend my protagonist’s plan (and entire life)?

Ari finds out both himself and Lila were born as experiments. The agency worked semi-alongside the Sovari lab and Ari and Lila's parents. They discover the reason Ari and Lila are wanted by the agency.

- Why does it matter to my protagonist, given their desire and fear?

he wants to be free, he doesn't want to be stuck in a lab.

- How is it going to change the game for my protagonist?

he realises the power he has

POST-MIDPOINT ACTION HERO

PROTAGONIST APPROACHES THEIR GOAL FROM A DIFFERENT ANGLE

- What is my protagonist’s NEW plan going to be, based on the game-changing midpoint and what it means to them?

After learning he and Lila are experiments and the agency wants to exploit them, Ari’s goal shifts: it’s no longer just about escaping Brax or surviving in the short term. His new plan is to keep Lila safe, evade the agency, and find a way to uncover the truth about who they are and why they were made, while staying ahead of anyone trying to control them.

- How does my protagonist think their new plan is going to actually work and bring them to their goal while STILL avoiding their fear?

Ari believes the best way to avoid being captured is to stay mobile, rely on the loyalty of his friends, and keep the agency off-balance. He thinks that by acting quickly, making small but decisive moves, and staying close to Lila, he can protect both of them without directly confronting the agency head-on—avoiding immediate danger while buying time to figure out a bigger solution.

ACT 3

SECOND PINCH POINT

OPPOSITION/ANTAGONISTIC FORCE GETS CLOSER

   - How can I show the opposition/antagonistic force now getting closer to disrupting the protagonist’s life?

  • The agency closing in on Ari and friends.

  • Ari realises staying hidden won’t protect them.

  • Foreshadow that he may have to make a personal sacrifice.

  • agency closing in aggressively, they are omnipresent and smarter than Ari expected.

  • Ari’s friends are **directly threatened, maybe Dom or Kai gets cornered.

  • Ari realises hiding or running isn’t enough; he can’t just avoid danger.

  • Raise emotional stakes: his fear of losing people he cares about intensifies, making him question his ability to protect anyone.

  • Could include a smaller betrayal or misstep that shows his misbelief (“if I don’t obey or plan perfectly, people get hurt”) still influences him.

**SUPPOSED VICTORY

PROTAGONIST THINKS THEY HAVE OVERCOME

PROMPT:

Ask yourself:

- Why does my protagonist think they will be victorious?

He outsmarts the agency.

- What’s happening to make them feel SO CLOSE to achieving their goal and finding happiness at last?

idk

  • Ari thinks he’s finally outsmarted the agency, maybe by creating a temporary safe route, distracting them,.
  • The victory is partial, showing competence and courage, giving him hope.
  • But it sets up overconfidence, making him underestimate the agency’s resources or foresight.
  • Could include a small “proof” that his powers or plan are working, like using his healing or clever planning to save someone.
  • This is the calm before the storm — he feels close to success, but the disaster is looming.

Successful Escape / Diversion:

  • Ari uses his clever planning to get Lila or friends out of immediate danger.
  • Maybe he creates a diversion, tricks the agency briefly, or rescues someone.
  • Feels like a win, but the agency still has resources and knowledge, so the threat isn’t gone.

DISASTER

EVERYTHING GOES WRONG

Right when they think they've made it out Dom and Kai get captured.

  • What does this disaster specifically mean to my protagonist?

His friends are important to him.

  • How does it force them to realize that they’re the one to blame for this crisis?

Somehow his impulsiveness caused this

- How does it completely disarm them and make them face off with their fear and misbelief?

They realise that he is the one they want, him being with them is putting them in danger.

  • Dom and Kai get captured just when Ari thinks they’re safe.
  • Ari realises his impulsiveness led to this crisis.
  • He feels guilt and responsibility, believing he’s the danger to everyone he cares about.

DARK MOMENT

PROTAGONIST IS HOPELESS

- How is my protagonist forced into the ring with their kryptonite?

He decides to turn himself in to protect his friends, Lila decides to go with him, so he wont face it alone.

Inspired by when Omega gets captured in The Bad Batch.

Ari and Lila live out their days in the facility, big time skip, a few months go by.

  • Ari decides to turn himself in to protect his friends.
  • Lila chooses to go with him, so he’s not alone.
  • He faces his kryptonite: being in the agency’s hands and powerless.
  • Time skip: Ari and Lila spend months in captivity, feeling trapped and hopeless.

The facility has more of the beasts that Brax cared for years ago.

Ari has bonded with one of them in secret and can somewhat communicate with it.

AHA MOMENT

RENEWED HOPE

Ari's friends find a way to break into the facility and rescue him and Lila.

- How is my protagonist going to overcome their fear and continue to the climax, therefore developing as a character?

while he thought complying was the only way to keep his friends safe, he realises they still love him and will help him even if it means putting themselves in danger, they will share Ari's burden and Ari doesn't have to take it on alone.

- What lesson are they going to learn (and simultaneously teach the audience?)

that friendship is powerful yayyy

  • Dom and Kai break into the facility to rescue Ari and Lila.
  • Ari realises he doesn’t have to bear responsibility alone; his friends will share the burden.
  • Lesson/Theme: friendship and trust are powerful, and courage can be shared.

CLIMACTIC CONFRONTATION

PROTAGONIST FACES THEIR BIGGEST CHALLENGE YET

- How does my protagonist prove their transformation by crushing their misbelief and facing their greatest fear with courage?

idk somehow in the escape he chooses to follow his on beliefs rather than orders, he escapes with his friends

While they are escaping everyone gets caught except for Ari.
He's given the option to turn himself in and his friends are set free, or if he leaves his friends will be killed.

Obviously the only reasonable option is to turn himself in, but he pretends to, and right at the right moment he does something to destroy the lab and escape with friends, destroying of lab maybe smth hes been working on in secret all these months idk how

  • Everyone else is captured except Ari.
  • Ari is given a choice: turn himself in and save friends or leave and risk their deaths.
  • He pretends to comply but secretly has a plan he’s been preparing for months.
  • He destroys the lab/facility with the help of the beast, the same way Brax did so many years later, but this time it didn't kill anyone important, escaping with his friends.
  • Proves he can trust his instincts and take action without blindly following orders.
  • Shows courage and leadership, crushing his misbelief that “if I disobey, people will get hurt.”

VICTORY

PROTAGONIST OVERCOMES

This doesn’t necessarily mean the protagonist wins the external battle or that things turn out exactly how they hoped. But the protagonist does experience a personal victory — because they overcame their fear and destroyed their misbelief. Here’s my rule of thumb: if your reader doesn’t know how the character has transformed as a result of their journey, you need to rewrite your book until they do.

- How has my protagonist changed as a result of their journey?

He isnt as quick to comply with peoples orders, he does want is best for both him and his friends.

RESOLUTION

WRAPPING UP LOOSE ENDS

- Where will my protagonist go from here?

Maybe he goes back and frees the scrappers, or just his brother.

- What does their life look like, now that they have overcome their misbelief?

The agency is no longer after them, he has no overarching figures.

possible hint at romance between Ari and Lila, or a full on confession if I decide to drop hints throughout the book instead
